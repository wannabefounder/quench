# Quench funding and fiscal-sponsorship packet

This document prepares Quench for funding without claiming an application, approval, partnership,
or donation channel that does not exist.

## Application summary

**Project:** Quench  
**Repository:** https://github.com/wannabefounder/quench  
**License:** MIT  
**Mission:** Make the estimated water footprint of personal AI use understandable, private, and
actionable through a friendly hydration race.

Quench is a native macOS app that ingests count-only metadata from local AI-tool logs, explicitly
connected provider APIs, an optional Chromium companion, or an off-by-default foreground-time
fallback. It calculates water estimates locally with a versioned EcoLogits-faithful methodology,
shows honest scopes and regions, and never needs conversation content or a Quench account. The
project combines environmental literacy, personal wellbeing, and inspectable open-source tooling.

## Why fiscal hosting fits

A fiscal host can provide transparent accounting, receive grants or donations, pay approved project
expenses, and handle legal/financial administration without Quench forming a company on day one.
Open Source Collective remains a plausible fit and currently states a 10% fee on incoming funds.
Acceptance is discretionary and does not create a charity partnership.

## Readiness against current OSC criteria

- **Original, actively developed software:** ready; public repository and commit history.
- **OSI-approved license:** ready; MIT.
- **Contribution and conduct documents:** ready; `CONTRIBUTING.md` and `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`.
- **Published governance:** ready; `GOVERNANCE.md`.
- **Organizational repository:** not ready; the repository is under a personal GitHub account.
- **Preferably two administrators:** not ready; the project owner must appoint a second trusted admin.
- **Terms, fees, and community guidelines accepted:** owner action at application time.

## Owner actions before applying

1. Revoke the GitHub token exposed during development and enable account 2FA.
2. Create a Quench GitHub organization, add at least one trusted administrator, and transfer the
   repository only after reviewing the ownership and permissions impact.
3. Protect `main`, require CI, enable secret scanning and push protection, and restrict release
   credentials.
4. Read the current fiscal-sponsorship terms, fee schedule, eligibility criteria, and community
   guidelines.
5. Submit through Open Source Collective's GitHub or manual verification flow using the application
   summary above. Record the resulting agreement and administrators publicly if approved.

Official starting points:

- https://docs.oscollective.org/interested-in-joining-osc/acceptance-criteria
- https://docs.oscollective.org/how-to-apply

## Funds-use policy draft

If sponsorship is approved, permitted expenses should directly support Quench's public mission:

- Apple Developer membership, signing/notarization, hosting, and release infrastructure;
- accessibility, security, privacy, and independent methodology review;
- design, documentation, localization only if the product owner reopens that scope, and community
  contributor work approved through a public issue;
- research datasets or specialist review needed to improve open coefficients;
- outreach and community events with a published purpose and budget.

Budgets and paid relationships should be public through the fiscal host. Personal expenses,
undisclosed provider promotion, and pass-through charity fundraising are outside scope.

## Grants

The 2026 research report names a Mozilla AI & Environmental Justice award, but a current official
open call could not be verified during the July 18, 2026 audit. Treat it as a lead to monitor, not an
available grant. Before any application, verify the live call, eligibility, deadline, legal-entity
requirements, permitted geography, reporting obligations, and intellectual-property terms from the
funder's official site. The application narrative above can be adapted only after those facts exist.
